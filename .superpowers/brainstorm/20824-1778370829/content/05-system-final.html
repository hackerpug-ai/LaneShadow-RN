The Three Tiers

Tier 1 — Favicon

16px → 48px

Sun, horizon, road. Three primary forms. The Bauhaus minimum.

Used for: browser favicons, app icon at small sizes, mention pills, tiny brand marks anywhere on map screens.

Tier 2 — Medallion (primary)

48px → 1024px

Adds the rolling-hills silhouette. The literary register kicks in. Hand-inked, imperfect, warm-paper.

Used for: the iOS/Android app icon, splash screen, in-app brand mark, marketing OG images, social avatars.

Tier 3 — Heritage Badge

1024px+ / merchandise

Medallion wrapped in double-ring seal, arc-text wordmark + tagline. The patch.

Used for: marketing posters, the about page, t-shirts, stickers, eventual merch, App Store store-listing hero, business cards.

Locked decisions

  • ✅ Emotion: ESCAPE — "the bike is the only honest answer the week ever gave you"
  • ✅ Visual archetype: Setting sun + horizon + road wedge in classical perspective
  • ✅ Format system: three-tier identity (Favicon → Medallion → Heritage Badge)
  • ✅ Medallion contents: sun, horizon, rolling hills silhouette, road — no rider (the rider is the viewer)
  • ✅ Color discipline: ink #1F1A14, paper #FDFBF8, copper #D9742A — copper has narrative job (it IS the sun)
  • ✅ Hand quality: imperfection earned by craft — slight wobble, ink-trap at convergence, weight modulation in road wedge
  • ✅ Voice: WPA park-poster meets 1960s field guide. Patient, restrained, instantly legible.
