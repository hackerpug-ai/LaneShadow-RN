Last visual decision: what's in the medallion?

The medallion's structure is locked (sun, horizon, road, warm paper, copper accent). But two details shift the brand's voice. Once we pick, I write the spec and we move to implementation.

1 — With the rider

A human in the frame. Tiny, far ahead, mid-ride.

What it adds: immediate "this is about people who ride." A rider sees themselves in the mark — tiny against the bigness of out there. The romance of the lonely rider in big country.

What it costs: the rider becomes a fleck below 64px and disappears at 32px. Forces a "favicon variant" that drops the rider. More moving parts in the composition.

Voice: literary, narrative, Blue Highways. Annie Proulx's Wyoming.

2 — Just the place

No rider. Sun, horizon, hills, road. The frame is yours to fill.

What it adds: the rider is you, the viewer — about to enter the frame. Cleaner composition. Stronger silhouette. Better at every size.

What it costs: loses the explicit "people who ride" cue. Could read as a generic landscape mark for travel/road-trip apps.

Voice: patient, restrained, the WPA park-poster register. The kind of mark that ages without effort.

3 — Just the icon

No hills, no rider. Sun, horizon, road. The Bauhaus version.

What it adds: maximum simplicity. The favicon and the primary mark are the same artifact. Like Sagi Haviv's NBC peacock or Chase octagon — three forms, no padding.

What it costs: loses the "place" feeling. The medallion stops being a literary medallion and becomes a clean modern icon. Closer to a tech logo than a field-journal stamp.

Voice: modern, confident, restrained. Less Sibley, more Vignelli.

My recommendation

2 — Just the place. The rider in #1 is romantic but fragile (gone below 64px). The pure icon in #3 abandons the literary register that gives the brand its difference. #2 is the right middle: keeps the WPA-poster soul (rolling hills, atmosphere, sense of somewhere), holds together at every size, and the tiny rider can come back for marketing artwork (the badge, posters, t-shirts) where it has room to live. The mark says "there's a place out there waiting for you" — and the viewer is the rider, by implication.

What I'm asking

Pick one (click), or override. If "2 + the rider only on the badge tier" feels right, that's exactly the recommendation — confirm and I write the spec. If you want a hybrid I haven't sketched, say so.
