Design Rationale

The mark is two things at once: a winding road and its shadow on asphalt. The copper stroke is the lane — the path the rider chooses. The dark stroke is the shadow — always present, always following, slightly behind and below.

The negative space between them is where the motorcycle lives. You never see the rider. You see what the rider leaves behind: a lane, and its shadow. That's the name. That's the product. That's the mark.

The S-curve carries three meanings simultaneously: the shape of a good motorcycle road, the letter S (Shadow), and forward momentum through a turn. Bass always insisted a mark should work harder than decoration — it should mean something at every level of reading.

Two strokes. Two colors. No fills. No gradients. No ornamentation. It faxes clean. It embroiders clean. It works at 16 pixels and on a billboard. That's the test.
