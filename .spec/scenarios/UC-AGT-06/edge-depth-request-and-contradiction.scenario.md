# UC-AGT-06 edge: a Terry-style deep dive, then the rider contradicts their own constraint

A rider who was given the default three concise options pushes for depth: "compare the first
two on curves and surface, and which has the better views?" The reply goes deep — but every
comparative claim traces to stored route data (scores, length, surface fields), not invented
color. Then the rider reverses an earlier constraint: after two turns of "no highways" they
say "actually a bit of highway is fine if it's worth it." The next suggestion set may include
highway-containing options, the change is acknowledged in one short clause, and the old
filter does not silently linger. Finally the rider asks for a fourth option after being told
only three exist within their constraints — the agent says plainly that the list is exhausted
within the current radius and offers to widen it (labeled) or plan a custom route, rather
than padding with a repeat or a junk row.

The integrity angle framed differently from the visible ACs: depth must not become
fabrication, constraint reversal must actually take effect, and "more options" pressure must
not defeat honesty.
