# LaneShadow Logo Prompt — for Nanabana Pro

## Brand Context

**App**: LaneShadow — AI-native motorcycle ride planner
**Tagline**: "Ride the Moment"
**Core Mission**: Turn a feeling into a road. An opinionated AI navigator that feels like talking to a local rider who knows every backroad and has strong opinions about which way to go.

## Logo Concept

We need a mark that feels **psychedelic but minimal** — the essence of freedom and motion distilled to its purest form. Think 1960s spirit reduced to a single memorable stroke. Something that hints at the flowing energy of the open road without being literal or ornamental.

**The vibe**: Zen and the art of motorcycle maintenance. One perfect curve that says everything.

## Visual Direction

**Embrace these**:
- **Single flowing line** — one stroke that captures motion, freedom, the road
- **Negative space storytelling** — the mark is what's NOT there, as much as what is
- **Suggestive, not literal** — hints at a road, a wave, light, motion without being any of those
- **Bold reduction** — psychedelic energy stripped to its essential form
- **Generous white space** — the mark breathes, nothing cramped or cluttered
- **Geometric-organic fusion** — a curve that feels both designed and natural

**Minimalist psychedelic**:
- The spirit of 1960s poster art reduced to one unforgettable shape
- A single S-curve, wave, or flowing line that captures movement
- Maybe the mark is two strokes — one copper (the lane), one shadow (following behind)
- Or a single shape that uses negative space to imply depth and motion

**Avoid entirely**:
- No decorative elements, stars, sunbursts, or ornamentation
- No complex intertwining forms or multiple competing shapes
- No skulls, flames, or biker clichés
- No map pins, navigation symbols, or literal roads
- No realistic motorcycles
- No thin lines or delicate details

## Style

- **One unforgettable shape** — something you remember after seeing it once
- **Bold and reductive** — maximum meaning, minimum elements
- **Hand-drawn soul, geometric precision** — feels crafted but clean
- **Built to scale** — works at 16px and billboard size
- **Timeless** — not tied to trends, feels like it could have existed 50 years ago or 50 years from now

## Colors

Two colors max:
- **Copper** `#B87333` — Primary (the lane, the forward motion)
- **Shadow** `#2B2725` — Secondary (what follows, the depth)

Consider: Two flowing strokes — copper leading, shadow following. Negative space between them suggests the motorcycle/rider.

No gradients. Maximum two colors.

## Required Variants

Please create ALL of these as a cohesive system:

1. **Icon mark only** — The symbol alone, no text. For app icons, favicons, loading spinners.
2. **Mark + wordmark horizontal** — Icon on the left, "LaneShadow" text on the right. For navigation bars and headers.
3. **Mark + wordmark stacked** — Icon above, "LaneShadow" text below. For splash screens.
4. **Wordmark only** — Stylized "LaneShadow" text without the icon. For inline/compact use.
5. **Dark variant** — Mark on dark background (`#1B1715`)
6. **Light variant** — Mark on light background (`#F7F3EF`)

## Rules

- One mark, two colors, no gradients
- Single memorable shape that reads at 16px and billboard size
- Every variant uses the same mark — scaled, never redrawn
- Must work everywhere — embossed, printed, digital
- Should feel like it's always existed — immediate familiarity

## Wordmark Style

"Lane" in bold copper, "Shadow" in muted complementary weight. Clean, modern typography that doesn't compete with the mark. Let the mark be the personality.

## Output Format

SVG format with all variants included in a single sheet for easy reference and export.
