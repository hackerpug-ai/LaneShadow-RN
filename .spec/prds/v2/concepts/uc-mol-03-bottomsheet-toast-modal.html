§01

Purpose & Atomic Composition

Three overlay molecules built from surface tokens and LSButton (UC-ATM-02). LSBottomSheet covers contextual action panels and detail drawers at three detent depths. LSToast delivers transient feedback using color.status.* semantics — no hard-coded colors. LSModal centers a card dialog for blocking confirmation flows.

All three reference motion.recipe.chatOverlayEnter (320ms spring enter) and motion.recipe.chatOverlayDismiss (auto-dismiss at 5000ms) by name. Motion parameters are not inlined; they are resolved at runtime from the token system. Dark-mode surface tokens swap automatically via the .mode-dark override.

§03

States & Variants

All overlay molecules support a dark-mode surface swap via .mode-dark. In dark mode: surface.overlay → rgba(45,34,24,0.92), surface.card → ink-700 (#2D2218). Status toast colors remain unchanged — they are semantic and independent of the surface theme.

LSBottomSheet dismiss on swipe-down: sheet translates off-screen bottom in 240ms, scrim fades out. onDismiss fires exactly once after the animation completes, not on gesture initiation.

§05

Motion Recipes

chatOverlayEnter — enter animation for all overlay molecules. Sheet slides up from translateY(100%) to 0; modal scales from 0.94 to 1.0 while fading opacity 0 to 1. Duration: 320ms, easing: cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1) (spring feel without physics overhead). Scrim fades from 0 to target opacity simultaneously.

chatOverlayDismiss — serves dual purpose: (1) auto-dismiss timer at 5000ms for LSToast; (2) exit animation recipe for all overlay molecules when manually dismissed. Exit: reverse of enter, 240ms ease-in. Swipe-to-dismiss on bottom sheet follows the gesture velocity — if velocity exceeds threshold the dismiss fires even if detent threshold was not crossed.

phaseDotPulse (reference) — not used at this molecule tier but composed by UC-MOL-07. Listed here to distinguish from overlay recipes.

§06

Rationale + AC Traceability

LSBottomSheet uses three fixed detents rather than free-form drag because LaneShadow's map-first layouts need predictable anchor points. Small is a teaser peek; medium is the primary content area; large exposes full detail. The 36pt drag handle is fixed (not tokenized) because it is a platform convention, not a brand decision — changing it would undermine native feel.

LSToast uses color.status.* semantic tokens so dark-mode tint swap is automatic. Hard-coding success green or warning amber directly would require a separate dark-mode override per toast variant.
