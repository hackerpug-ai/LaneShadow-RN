§01 · Purpose

Conversations, not logs.

LSSessionsDrawer is the left-anchored conversation-history panel that slides over the dimmed map on SessionsScreen. It organizes sessions by recency, marks the active session with a copper stripe, and lets riders tap to restore any previous conversation complete with its map, route, and Navigator messages.

The drawer is always left-anchored — never bottom-modal — so the map behind it remains partially visible. The scrim is intentionally lighter (0.35 opacity) to keep spatial orientation while browsing.

§06 · Rationale

History, not
a list.

Design Decision

Sessions are organized as conversations, not as "saved routes." A route is the output; the session is the negotiation. This distinction drives the preview text being the opening prompt (italic Newsreader) rather than a route stat.

The 3px copper stripe on the active row communicates "you are here" — the current session — without any label. The tinted background reinforces it but the stripe alone is sufficient even at small scale.

The drawer is always left-anchored because the map behind it stays visible on the right side. This keeps the rider spatially oriented while reviewing sessions.
