§01 · Purpose

A conversation, not a log.

Sessions is the one place where the Navigator's relationship with the rider persists across time. Every ride starts as a conversation — a prompt, a planning phase, a set of routes, a choice. This screen remembers those conversations as sessions, grouped by recency, each labeled with the rider's named result ("Santa Cruz loop") and a one-line italic preview of the opening prompt.

A copper active-stripe + tinted row background marks the session the rider is currently in — the one whose map is dimmed behind the drawer. Tapping a non-active row restores that conversation whole: its route, its Navigator message, its phase history. This is why sessions are organized as conversations, not as "saved routes" — a route is the output; the session is the negotiation.

The drawer is always left-anchored (never bottom-modal, never center-sheet) so the map behind it stays partially visible. The scrim is intentionally lighter than a full modal (0.35 opacity, warm-black tint) to keep the rider oriented while they pick.

§05 · Motion

Slide in, scrim up, dismiss on tap.

sidebarSlideIn
Hamburger tap (from UC-SCR-01)
Drawer translateX −300 → 0, 250ms ease-out. Scrim opacity 0 → 0.35 in parallel.
sidebarSlideIn (reverse)
Scrim tap OR edge swipe
Drawer translateX 0 → −300, 200ms ease-in. Scrim fades concurrently. onDismiss fires at 50% progress.
— (row press)
Session row tap
Row pressed state (color.signal.tint) in 120ms. onSelect fires on release. Drawer dismisses via sidebarSlideIn reverse with the newly-active session's canvas pre-mounted beneath.
— (section sticky)
Scroll inside session-list
Section labels stick to top of viewport when their first row passes under.
mapTapDismiss
Scrim tap
150ms linear scrim fade; onDismiss from parent.
§06 · Rationale

Why a drawer, not a feed.

Design principles honored

Sessions is not the app's home. It's a place the rider visits when they want to resume something. The drawer shape respects that — it's a navigation affordance, not a destination. The map stays partially visible behind the scrim so the rider doesn't forget they're in the middle of planning.

Each row treats the session like a sentence: a title ("Santa Cruz loop"), a prompt in italic prose, and a small mono meta line. That's enough context to decide whether to resume, without opening the session and finding out the wrong answer.

AC traceability

  • AC-01 Scrim 0.35 + drawer sliding in + "Rides" header + NEW + THIS WEEK + 5 rows (1 active) — S01.
  • AC-02 iOS + Android identical.
  • AC-03 onSelect fires on non-active tap.
  • AC-04 onNew fires on NEW tap.
  • AC-05 Scrim tap fires onDismiss; drawer reverses motion.recipe.sidebarSlideIn.
  • AC-06 Scroll keeps header/NEW/section labels sticky — S02.
  • AC-07 Light/dark re-renders — S04.
  • AC-08 SessionsMockProvider injects sessions + activeSessionId.
