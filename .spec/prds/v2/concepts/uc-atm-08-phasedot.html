§01 · Purpose

Machine-state made visible.

LSPhaseDot communicates position in a multi-step process. The Navigator's planning pipeline has five steps — scout, draw, check weather, score, rank. The rider does not need to know the names. They need to see progress. Three states give them that at a glance: hollow (waiting), copper-filled with a pulse ring (in progress), green-filled (complete).

The 10px diameter is intentional. The dot must be legible in a crowded row but not consume the visual weight of adjacent labels. The ring pulse adds temporal rhythm without requiring the rider to look away from the map.

§06 · Rationale

Why a 10px dot, not larger.

Design Intent

10px is the minimum legible diameter on both retina iOS (3x) and Android XXXHDPI screens. At 10px the dot sits comfortably between label lines in a compact indicator row without requiring the indicator to grow taller than two lines of text.

The ring-pulse at 1.5x scale means the effective visual bounding box is 40px — but only during animation. The ring is visually transparent by design; it does not occupy space in layout terms. PhaseIndicator reserves 40px per step slot to avoid clipping the animation.

AC Traceability

  • AC-1 LSPhaseDot(.pending) — hollow, border.strong 1px, 10px diameter
  • AC-2 LSPhaseDot(.active) — signal fill + phaseDotPulse ring, 900ms ease-in-out infinite
  • AC-3 LSPhaseDot(.done) — success fill, no animation
  • AC-4 Story family: Pending, Active (animated), Done on both platforms
  • AC-5 Tests verify active-state animation references motion.recipe.phaseDotPulse — no hardcoded duration
  • AC-6 Ring max-radius = 15px; bounding box 40px documented for UC-MOL-07
  • AC-7 PhaseIndicator composition demo: done/done/active/pending/pending with labels in instrument.sm
