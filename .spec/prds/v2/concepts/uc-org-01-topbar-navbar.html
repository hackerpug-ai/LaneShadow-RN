§01 · Purpose

Glass chrome above the map.

LSTopBar is the primary top chrome organism used on every Navigator screen — Idle, Planning, RouteResults, RouteDetails, and Error. It floats above the map as a row of chips backed by the warm surface at 90% alpha with a light 8pt backdrop blur, respecting the status-bar safe area. It exposes three slots: a leading hamburger chip, an optional centered title, and a trailing NEW chip. Both chips share the same geometry — 40pt tall, radius.md rounded rectangles — so they read as a single family against both light paper and night-mode maps.

LSNavBar is retained for modal-sheet and full-screen modal contexts. It composes LSToolbar with an optional filter-chip row or search slot. It does not appear on any Navigator map screen — it only surfaces inside sheets and modals.

§06 · Rationale

Why glass, not solid.

Design Decision

Both chips use LSGlassPanel(.chrome) rather than a solid surface so the map remains readable at the edges of the screen. A solid bar would create a hard horizon; the glass chips float above the terrain without interrupting the spatial reading of the map.

The circular hamburger and pill NEW chip are differentiated by shape so riders can identify them without reading labels — circle means navigation history, pill with icon means new session.
