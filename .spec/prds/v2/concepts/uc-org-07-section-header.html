§01 · Purpose

Named sections, once.

LSSectionHeader is the in-screen group title organism used across SessionsDrawer, catalog views, and any screen that groups content into named sections. It is the simplest organism in the tier — a title row with an optional trailing "See all" link and a configurable leading inset.

By centralizing this pattern here, every screen that needs a section header composes from the same organism rather than re-implementing its own title row. Three slot configurations cover all known usage: title only, title + see all, and caps label (no trailing, used inside drawers).

§05 · Motion

No motion — static chrome.

none
Section header renders
LSSectionHeader has no entrance animation. It renders in-place with its content. The containing screen or list owns any scroll or transition animations.
none
See all tap
No animation on the header itself. onTap fires once; the navigation transition is handled by the caller screen's router. A subtle press state (opacity 0.7) can be applied by the button layer.
§06 · Rationale

One header, everywhere.

Design Decision

Three title variants (title-only, title+see-all, caps-label) cover every known usage across the Navigator and catalog surfaces without requiring a separate component for each. The caps variant is specifically designed for drawer interiors where ALL-CAPS section labels feel appropriate (drawer sections are contextually grouped, not navigable).

The leading inset prop defaults to spacing.3 (12pt) — matching the app's standard content margin — but is overridable so screens with different grid margins can adapt without creating a new variant.

Signal copper for "See all" establishes a consistent tappable-link color. Riders learn copper = actionable text throughout the app.
