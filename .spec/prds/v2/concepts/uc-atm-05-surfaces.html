§01 · Purpose

Every molecule needs a surface to stand on.

The surface trio defines three distinct elevation registers. LSCard is elevated with a subtle shadow — used for route cards, session tiles, any content that must float above the page. LSPanel is flat — an inset container that clarifies structure without adding visual weight. LSGlassPanel is the translucent chrome floating above the map canvas: backdrop-blurred, slightly warm, and capable of wearing a 3px leading stripe when it needs to carry a message.

All three atoms consume only token-resolved values. Raw color literals and raw dp/pt values are rejected at the type level — every consumer must name a semantic token.

§06 · Rationale

Why three surfaces, not one.

Design Intent

Three surfaces correspond to three visual layers. Cards lift content above the page canvas. Panels group content within a card or screen zone without adding another visual layer. GlassPanels float above the map — they must be transparent enough to hint at the geography below while opaque enough to remain readable.

The callout variant was separated from chrome because the semantic weight differs. Chrome panels are ambient infrastructure; callout panels carry meaning — signal for Navigator voice, warning for errors. The 3px stripe communicates that distinction at a glance without requiring the rider to read.

AC Traceability

  • AC-1 LSCard: color.surface.card + radius.lg + elevation.2 + spacing.4
  • AC-2 LSPanel: flat, color.surface.primary, radius.md, spacing.3
  • AC-3 LSGlassPanel.chrome: backdrop-blur 13px, elevation.overlay
  • AC-4 LSGlassPanel.callout(signal): 3px leading stripe color.signal.default
  • AC-5 LSGlassPanel.callout(warning): 3px stripe color.status.warning.default
  • AC-6 padding override: .spacing3 / .spacing4 / .spacing5 — token-only
  • AC-7 backgroundColor override: .surfaceCard only — raw literals rejected
  • AC-8 Light + dark mode verified for all three atoms
