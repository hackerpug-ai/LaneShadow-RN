# User Personas

LaneShadow serves motorcycle riders who plan scenic routes and need reliable navigation regardless of cellular coverage. Our users range from daily commuters to cross-country adventurers.

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Adventure Rider** | Primary user who rides remote mountain routes with spotty cell coverage. Plans weekend day trips and multi-day adventure tours. Downloads large offline regions (800MB+) for peace of mind. |
| **Urban Commuter** | Daily rider who navigates city traffic and encounters dead zones in parking garages and tunnels. Budget-conscious with limited data plans. Downloads home metro region (50MB). |
| **Touring Rider** | Cross-country enthusiast who plans multi-state routes with waypoints. Needs multi-region coverage (4GB+) for extended trips. Values reliability and pre-trip planning capabilities. |

## Role Details

### Adventure Rider (Primary)

**Profile:** 32-year-old software engineer in Denver, CO. Rides a BMW R 1250 GS Adventure. 15,000+ miles annually through remote mountain terrain.

**Responsibilities:**
- Plan scenic mountain routes with elevation gain
- Download large offline regions (800MB+) for multi-day trips
- View weather overlays for safety at elevation
- Access route history for favorite rides

**Pain Points:**
- Current routing fails in remote areas with no cell service
- Cannot plan routes ahead of time without internet
- Data usage concerns when riding in roaming areas
- Weather changes mid-ride in mountains

**Goals:**
- Reliable routing regardless of internet connection
- Pre-trip planning at home on WiFi
- Cost-effective data usage
- Weather-aware routing for safety

**Quote:** "I don't ride to get somewhere. I ride to be somewhere. But I need to know I can find my way home, even when there's no cell service for 50 miles."

### Urban Commuter (Secondary)

**Profile:** 27-year-old barista in Portland, OR. Daily commuter on a Honda CB300R. Budget-conscious with limited data plan.

**Responsibilities:**
- Plan daily commuting routes
- Navigate around traffic efficiently
- Check weather for rain gear decisions
- Minimize cellular data usage

**Pain Points:**
- Routing fails in parking garages and tunnels
- Data budget tight (2GB/month)
- GPS dead zones in urban areas
- Weather-aware routing needed

**Goals:**
- Reliable commuting routing
- Minimal data usage
- Quick rerouting around traffic
- Weather warnings for gear decisions

### Touring Rider (Power User)

**Profile:** 45-year-old consultant from Austin, TX. Cross-country tours on a Harley-Davidson Road Glide. Leads groups of 15+ riders.

**Responsibilities:**
- Plan multi-state routes with waypoints
- Download 10+ regions for tours (4GB+)
- Share routes with riding groups
- Coordinate multi-day trip logistics

**Pain Points:**
- Cannot plan multi-state routes offline
- Large region downloads needed
- Route sharing with group
- Weather across long distances

**Goals:**
- Pre-trip planning for entire tours
- Multi-region offline coverage
- Route sharing capabilities
- Long-range weather forecasts

**Quote:** "When I'm leading a group of 15 riders through 8 states, I don't have time to mess with GPS failures. The route needs to work, period."

## User Journey Examples

### Journey 1: Adventure Rider Plans Remote Day Trip

1. **At home (WiFi)**: Opens LaneShadow, asks AI for "scenic mountain loop with elevation gain"
2. **Route generation**: System generates 3 route options immediately (5-10s)
3. **Weather enrichment**: Weather badges fade in showing conditions at elevation (20s)
4. **Region check**: System notices no offline region for Rocky Mountains
5. **Download prompt**: "This route enters area with spotty coverage. Download Rocky Mountain region (800MB)?"
6. **User downloads**: Region downloads on WiFi (5-10 minutes)
7. **Confirmation**: "Region ready. You'll have full navigation even without cell service."
8. **Day of ride**: In remote mountains, routing works perfectly despite no cell service
9. **Mid-ride weather check**: Pulls over, checks updated weather for next 2 hours
10. **Post-ride**: Saves route to favorites, shares with riding group

### Journey 2: Urban Commuter Handles Dead Zones

1. **Parking garage**: Cell service cuts out underground
2. **Route request**: Asks AI for "fastest way home avoiding highway"
3. **Offline routing**: System calculates route using cached Portland region (3s)
4. **Weather check**: Shows current conditions and 1-hour forecast
5. **Tunnel navigation**: Routing continues smoothly through tunnel
6. **Traffic reroute**: Suggests alternate based on real-time traffic
7. **Arrival**: Arrives home, data usage minimal (<5MB)
